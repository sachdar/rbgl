Dangerous Goods Handling Expertise

RBGL ensures safe, compliant, and efficient transport of hazardous cargo across all major DG classes.

Full Container Booking of Dangerous Goods

RBGL ensures safe and compliant booking procedures for Dangerous Goods shipments. Below is the step-by-step process required for full container bookings:

Step 1 — Advance Submission of Documents

All required documents must be submitted at least 3 to 4 working days before shipment dispatched:

  • Booking Note for Dangerous Goods
  • Packaging Certification (Certification for Transport of Dangerous Goods verifying single-use packaging)
  • Material Safety Data Sheet (MSDS)

Important Notes:

  • Booking Note must include description in English, container type, DG Class No., UN Code, packaging details, and special requirements.
  • If transshipment is required, confirm restrictions with the carrier at the transshipment port.
  • Provide accurate consignment data: package count, net weight, gross weight, and volume.

Step 2 — Declaration Submission

Alongside the booking, submit complete declaration information:

  • Packaging Certification (each product description must match one-to-one)
  • MSDS for Dangerous Goods
  • Electronic Customs Declaration Documents & Power of Attorney (paperless clearance nationwide):
    • Contract
    • Invoice
    • Packing List
    • Customs Power of Attorney
    • Export Declaration

Step 3 — Loading & Shipping

Once documentation and declarations are verified, cargo is cleared for loading and onward shipment under RBGL’s compliance standards.
